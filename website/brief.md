# Notebound — Launch Website Brief

## What Notebound Is

A notebook app. Local-only software that you own. A mental workspace for note taking and study.

It follows the same skeuomorphic design principles as OneNote 2010 — using it feels like having a bookshelf and a desk and a stack of papers and binders. Information has hierarchy and spatial meaning. Notebooks, sections, pages, subpages. Not every page has equal relevance. There are orders and levels of importance.

## Who It's For

Thinkers and researchers. People who organise information for a living or because they can't help it. Not project managers. Not team leads. Not everyone.

## What It's Against

Cloud enshittification. Software that changes its UI every time you open it. Marketing popups on your own computer. Your machine is not a billboard — it's an extension of your mental workspace.

We miss the old software. We miss spatial tools. We just want to organise information on a screen.

## Website Principles

- No marketing BS. Say what it is and what it does.
- Show the product. Don't describe it with adjectives.
- Respect the visitor. No popups, no newsletter nags, no cookie banners.
- The site itself should feel like the software — calm, spatial, purposeful.
- One page. Download link. Done.

## Site Structure (single page)

1. **Hero** — Name, one-line description, screenshot, download button.
2. **The Problem** — Why we built this. The rant. Keep it honest and short.
3. **What You Get** — Notebooks, sections, pages, subpages. Freeform canvas. Local storage. Your data, your machine. No account needed.
4. **Screenshot / Demo** — Show the actual UI. No mockups, no illustrations.
5. **Download** — Platform links. That's it.

## Tone

Direct. Honest. Slightly angry. Not cute. Not corporate. Write like you're explaining it to a friend who's also fed up.
